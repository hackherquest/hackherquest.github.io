The event will take place on Saturday 15th of February, on the campus of Radboud University in Nijmegen. There will be lunch for registered participants. However, we cannot garantee that there will be dinner in the evening. Please inform us of any dietary restrictions when you sign up through the Google form.

Because the event promotes women in Cyber Security, you have to identify as a female student to participate in any of the events.

Hackathon (CTF)

This event is only for Dutch and international HBO and WO students, who are enrolled at a Dutch educational institution.

We have space for 32 HBO/WO students. You will have to sign-up in groups of 4. If you want to sign-up with less, you will be placed in a group with other students.

The Capture The Flag (CTF) competition is a cybersecurity competition where participants solve challenges to find hidden flags. The challenges are based on real-world scenarios and are designed to test the participants' knowledge and skills in various cybersecurity domains. The competition is open to all skill levels, but the challenges will lean more towards beginner and intermediate levels. The competition will be held on-site and will last for 3,5 hours. There will be prizes for the winners.

Students who sign up for the hackathon, will have the oppertunity to participate in one of the 2-hour workshops that will be held in the morning. They can choose between the following workshops: Introduction to Hardware Hacking, Introduction to Cryptography, and Introduction to Z3 solving. The workshops are designed for beginners and are on-site.

Complete beginners have the oppertunity to join the Introduction to Web Security workshop, in which case they will join the High School students in the morning session.

Treasure hunt

This event is only for Dutch high school students, who are enrolled at a Dutch high school in Nijmegen.

We have space for 20 high school students. You will have to sign-up in groups of 4. If you want to sign-up with less, you will be placed in a group with other students.

For high school students, there is a treasure hunt that will take place on the campus of Radboud University. The treasure hunt will last for 3,5 hours and will take place in parallel to the CTF competition in the afternoon. The treasure hunt will consist of various puzzels provided by Challenge The Cyber. There will be prizes for the winners.

High school students who sign up for the treasure hunt, will have the oppertunity to participate in the Introduction to Web Security workshop in the morning. The workshop is designed for beginners and is on-site.
