Introduction to Hardware Hacking

Dr I.R. Buhan (Ileana)

Dr. I.R. Buhan (Ileana) is an assistant professor at the Institute for Computing and Information Sciences at Radboud University. She is a member of the Digital Security group and CESCA Lab. Her main research interest is hardware security, emphasizing the advancement of tools to create secure hardware.

She will give an introduction to hardware hacking, in which you will learn the basics of hardware hacking and how to get started.

This workshop is suitable for (applied) university students.

Introduction to Web Security

BSc. Anca Strulea
assisted by MSc. Danique Lummen

Anca is bright and ambitious student from the Vrij Universiteit Amsterdam (VU), who is currently doing her master in Computer Security. One of her biggest accomplishments is getting selected as one of the 40 students to receive a scholarship from ASML. Anca has always been passionate about sharing her knowledge with others, which is why she will be given a workshop on the Introduction to Web Security. During which she will how to intercept traffic, and if time permits some basic web vulnerabilities.

She will be assisted by Danique Lummen, who is currently working as a Cyber Security Specialist at Schiphol. In 2022 she was the Team Captain of Team Europe in the International Cyber security Challenge (ICC), competing in a two-day hacking capture the flag competition against other continents and countries. Her task was specifically focused on forensics and team management. They managed to secure first place for the jeopardy day, as well as first place for the overall competition. Furthermore, Danique is a volunteer for the Challenge The Cyber organisation. This organisation is responsible for the yearly National Capture The Flag (CTF) competition in the Netherlands.

The workshop is suitable for both highschool and (applied) university students.

Introduction to Cryptography

BSc. Aliza Reif

Aliza is a master student at Radboud University, specializing in Cyber Security and Artificial Intelligence. She is currently doing her internship at the German Aerospace Center (DLR) in the AI-Security institute, where she is conducting research on an adversarial data poisoning with the goal to mount the attack in a real-world setting.

Furthermore, Aliza has been an active member of the Capture The Flag team of Radboud University. She loves to do Cryptography challenges, and will use this knowledge to guide you through her introduction to cryptography. In this workshop you will learn the basic structures of cryptography.

This workshop is suitable for both highschool and (applied) university students.
